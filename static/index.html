About the project: The development of an inexpensive solar irradiance monitoring system.

This project is dedicated to the Group Project course in the final year of USTH Energy students.

The main concern of this project is to develop a low-cost solar irradiance monitoring system. The idea is to measure the illuminance (express in lux), then calculate the Global Horizontal Irradiance (express in W/m2). Illuminance is a measure of photometric flux per unit area or visible flux density, and is typically expressed in lux (lumens per square meter). Luminous flux is a measure of power of visible light. Photopic flux, expressed in lumens, is weighted to match the responsibility of the human eye, which is most sensitive to yellow-green. Irradiance is a measure of radiometric flux per unit area or flux density, and is typically expressed in Watt per square meter. The relationship between illuminance and irradiance is often found to be linear. Hence, the illuminance measurement could serve as an indirect measurement of irradiance.

Illuminance sensor GY-49 (MAX44009) is used to measure the illuminance, and sensor DHT11 is used to measure the temperature and humidity. GY-49 is chosen because of its wide range of measurement, from 0 to 188,000 lux, making it suitable for measuring outdoor illuminance. DHT11 is popular in IoT application thanks to its low-cost and easy-to-use characteristics.

Module ESP8266 NODEMCU acts as the micro-controller as well as the communication module. ESP8266 is made for wireless communication purpose using 2.4GHz wifi connection. The ESP8266 NODEMCU module is integrated with GPIO pins, making it able to read digital and analog input (micro-controller capability), and also CP2102 module in order to upload code to the micro-controller through USB connection. About this website

This website is orgranized into 3 pages: Home, Charts, Table. The Homepage (this page) includes general information about the project. The Charts page displays the collected data in the form of line graph, while the Tables page display them in the form of table. The data on both pages are updated in real-time. The raw data could be downloaded on the Tables page in csv and excel format.
